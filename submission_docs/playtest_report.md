# Magic Rush — Playtest Reports

---

## Playtest 1 — Willy

**Date:** 10 March 2026
**Tester Profile:** Husband, hedge fund portfolio manager, age 35. Regular console gamer — FIFA, strategy games. Comfortable with complex UI and multi-tasking under time pressure.
**Session length:** ~12 minutes (3 playthroughs)
**Build version:** V8 (post Chi rebalance, pre audio)

### What Worked
- Immediately grasped the two-room structure without explanation. Moved between Sanctuary and Living Room fluidly within the first run.
- Appreciated the three-ending system: "The fact that you can actually WIN-win by hitting 100 changes how you play. I was actively trying to silence the Baby to get the +40 rather than just surviving."
- Found the 60-second Baby wake cycle predictable in a good way: "It's like a shot clock. You know it's coming, so you pace your crafting around it."
- Chi drain at 2/sec was described as "tight but fair — like managing a burning clock, not a death sentence."

### What Didn't Work
- Dragging items onto NPCs missed frequently because he was clicking the center of the NPC sprite rather than the body hitbox edge. "The grab area doesn't feel where I'd expect it from a console game."
- The three-step Baby Seal recipe caused a full stop on the first run: "I had no idea Crystal was a thing I needed to make. I went back to Sanctuary and tried Obsidian + Candle because the names aren't on screen when I'm in Living Room."
- Suggested the backpack should show a "selected" glow ring rather than just a gold border: "It works, but it doesn't feel premium."

### Feature Requests (Willy)
- **Draft queue**: Pre-queue the next ingredient to grab while crafting, so there's no downtime between the craft completing and the next drag cycle. "FIFA has a skill move queue — same concept."
- **NPC priority indicator**: Show which NPC has been waiting longest, so experienced players can optimize delivery order rather than defaulting to clockwise.
- **Speedrun timer**: A secondary display showing personal best run time for Perfect Victory. "I'd replay just to beat my own record."

## Playtest 2 — Vivian

**Date:** 17 March 2026
**Tester Profile:** Close friend, photographer, age 28. Mobile-first gamer — Candy Crush, Merge games. Plays in short sessions, comfort with tap-and-swipe but unfamiliar with mouse-drag precision.
**Session length:** ~18 minutes (2 playthroughs + tutorial questions)
**Build version:** V12 (post font + three endings, pre drag-move fix)

### What Worked
- Title screen immediately legible and inviting. "The gold title looks like a real game. I would have kept scrolling past if it looked like a student project."
- Loved the Baby Siren audio: "The moment that sound came on I panicked in the right way. It really signals 'something is wrong.'"
- The three-ending screen visuals received strong positive feedback: "The deep red DEFEATED screen actually made me feel something. Most mini-games just say GAME OVER in boring text."
- Completed her first full three-minute run and achieved TIME'S UP on second attempt. Felt accomplishment from progress between runs.

### What Didn't Work
- Initial confusion about drag mechanics. Expected to tap the item and tap the destination (Candy Crush-style), not hold-drag. "I kept clicking instead of holding. The tutorial doesn't tell me I need to hold."
- Recipe panel in Sanctuary was too small to read comfortably. "I had to lean in. On a phone this would be unplayable." (Note: this was a desktop build; mobile adaptation was not in scope.)
- Couldn't figure out that the Baby required a 3-step craft — discovered it accidentally on second run: "I thought I was doing it wrong. I didn't know Obsidian was the thing."

### Feature Requests (Vivian)
- **Hold-drag visual tutorial on first launch**: A brief animated guide showing the press-hold-drag motion before the first playthrough begins. "Even just a hand animation doing it once would save so much confusion."
- **Recipe pop-up on item hover**: When hovering over an inventory item, show a small tooltip with its name and what it can combine with. "Like Candy Crush showing the candy type when you hold it."
- **Cute NPC idle animations**: "They walk but they don't do anything when you deliver correctly except flash. What if they jumped or waved? It would feel so much more alive."
- **Mobile layout option**: Larger tap targets, bigger icons, portrait mode support for phone play.

## Playtest 3 — Eva

**Date:** 20 March 2026
**Tester Profile:** Niece, student, age 12. iPad-native gamer — Fruit Ninja, Roblox, casual puzzle games. No mouse-and-keyboard experience. Plays games in 5–10 minute bursts.
**Session length:** ~25 minutes (4 playthroughs — hardest to stop)
**Build version:** V14 (post drag simplification, drag = move not copy)

### What Worked
- Fastest to learn the drag mechanic of all four testers. "Oh it's like Fruit Ninja but you DROP them." Adapted within 30 seconds.
- Immediately understood NPC delivery: watched the ! bubble appear and dragged to the NPC without prompting. "It's obvious you have to give them what they want."
- Found the Baby terrifying in a delightful way: "Is it going to get me? What happens if I don't stop it?" — strong emotional engagement with the antagonist.
- Replayed four times without being asked. Achieved TIME'S UP on run 3, then targeted PERFECT VICTORY on run 4 after being told it existed.

### What Didn't Work
- Got confused by the scene-transition arrow button (◀/▶) on first run: "I thought that was a back button and closed the game." Expected a text label or icon she recognized.
- The Sanctuary workstation area felt cluttered: "There's too much stuff on the left side. I didn't know where to put my things."
- Ran out of time before crafting the Baby Seal on every run except run 4. "You can't make it fast enough."

### Feature Requests (Eva)
- **Baby character animation**: "Can the baby actually move around? It just sits there. In Roblox everything moves." A wobble or bounce animation when the Baby wakes would increase perceived menace.
- **Combo bonus**: Deliver three correct items in a row and get a +5 Magic bonus. "Like a streak in Fruit Ninja."
- **Color-coded NPCs**: Give each NPC a distinct color halo so it's faster to match item colors to recipients. "The one in the purple hat always wants the purple thing, right?"
- **Difficulty levels**: Easy mode with longer timer and lower Baby drain, so younger players can complete a full run. "My friends would play this but it's too hard for them."

## Playtest 4 — Elvin

**Date:** 24 March 2026
**Tester Profile:** Family lawyer, age 52. Not a gamer. Uses computers daily for work but has no gaming frame of reference.
**Session length:** ~30 minutes (1 full playthrough + many questions)
**Build version:** V15 (final BGM, mark.png NPC icons)

### What Worked
- The How to Play screen was read carefully and found sufficient: "This tells me everything I need. I don't need a tutorial level — I can read."
- The SPACE to pause feature was discovered organically and used frequently: "I needed to stop and think. Glad I could."
- Achieved TIME'S UP on first attempt despite slow start — the forgiving initial Chi value (20) gave him time to learn without dying immediately.
- The DEFEATED screen with dark red made the failure state immediately legible: "I knew I'd lost. It's clear."

### What Didn't Work
- Spent 8 minutes in Sanctuary before going to Living Room because the scene-transition arrow was not prominent enough. "I assumed all the game was in this room."
- Dragging to NPCs was inconsistent — missed frequently due to unfamiliarity with click-and-hold mouse mechanics. "I kept letting go too early."
- Had no intuition for what items NPCs wanted. Would have preferred the ! icon to automatically show the request rather than requiring a click.
- Found the Chi Bar poorly explained: "I didn't know what it measured or what filling it meant until I read the rules again."

### Feature Requests (Elvin)
- **Auto-show NPC requests**: Display the speech bubble passively above each NPC without requiring a click. "Lawyers don't click around hoping to find information. Put it in front of them."
- **Chi Bar label**: A persistent text label next to the bar reading "Magic Meter" with current value "42 / 100" so non-gaming players understand the mechanic at a glance.
- **Clearer room transition**: A door visual or animated portal between the two rooms rather than an arrow button. "I'd understand a door. An arrow could mean anything."
- **End-screen play statistics**: Show total items crafted, total items delivered, and Baby silencings — not just time and Magic. "I want to know how I played, not just that I lost."

*All playtests conducted on MacOS desktop, 1280×720 resolution. Testers played without real-time guidance after initial setup.*
